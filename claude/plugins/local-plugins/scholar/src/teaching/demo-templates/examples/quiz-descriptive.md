## Question 1 (5 points) - Multiple Choice

Which measure of central tendency is most affected by extreme values (outliers)?

A) Mean
B) Median
C) Mode
D) All are equally affected

**Answer:** A) Mean

**Explanation:** The mean is calculated using all values in the dataset, so extreme values pull the mean toward them. The median only considers the middle position, and the mode only considers frequency, making them resistant to outliers.

## Question 2 (5 points) - Multiple Choice

For a dataset with values {2, 4, 4, 5, 7, 9, 9, 9, 10}, what is the mode?

A) 4
B) 5
C) 9
D) 6.56

**Answer:** C) 9

**Explanation:** The mode is the value that appears most frequently. In this dataset, 9 appears three times, more than any other value. The value 4 appears twice, and all others appear once.

**Difficulty:** Easy

## Question 3 (5 points) - True/False

The variance of a dataset can be negative.

**Answer:** False

**Explanation:** Variance is calculated as the average of squared deviations from the mean. Since squared numbers are always non-negative, variance can never be negative. The minimum value of variance is 0, which occurs only when all values in the dataset are identical.

**Difficulty:** Medium
**Learning Objective:** LO 1.3 - Understand measures of variability

## Question 4 (5 points) - Calculation

Calculate the standard deviation for the following sample: {10, 12, 14, 16, 18}

**Answer:** 3.16 (or $\sqrt{10}$)

**Solution Steps:**
1. Calculate mean: $\bar{x} = \frac{10+12+14+16+18}{5} = 14$
2. Calculate squared deviations:
   - $(10-14)^2 = 16$
   - $(12-14)^2 = 4$
   - $(14-14)^2 = 0$
   - $(16-14)^2 = 4$
   - $(18-14)^2 = 16$
3. Sum of squared deviations: $16+4+0+4+16 = 40$
4. Sample variance: $s^2 = \frac{40}{5-1} = 10$
5. Standard deviation: $s = \sqrt{10} \approx 3.16$

**Difficulty:** Medium
**Learning Objective:** LO 1.4 - Calculate standard deviation

## Question 5 (5 points) - Short Answer

A researcher reports that a dataset has a mean of 50 and a median of 65. What does this tell you about the shape of the distribution?

**Sample Answer:** The distribution is left-skewed (negatively skewed). When the mean is less than the median, extreme low values are pulling the mean downward. This indicates a longer tail on the left side of the distribution.
